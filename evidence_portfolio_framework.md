# Policy Advocacy Evidence Portfolio — Research Framework
**Dataset:** ~18,000 Media Cloud news articles (opioid prescribing policy, forced tapering, veterans' health, kratom/7-OH, scheduling actions, patient abandonment, surveillance data controversies)
**Prepared:** July 2026

**Stated assumptions about the dataset:**
1. Articles span roughly 2016–2026 (CDC 2016 guideline era through the 2025 7-OH scheduling push), with metadata including at minimum title, outlet, date, and URL.
2. Coverage mixes national wire/legacy outlets, local/regional papers, trade press, and advocacy-adjacent publications.
3. The dataset contains both articles sympathetic to pain-patient access arguments and articles supporting restriction; the framework below deliberately queries both.
4. Full text is available (not just headlines/abstracts), so quote extraction and named-individual queries are possible.

---

## OUTPUT 1: Exploratory Question Set (42 Questions)

### A. Patient Stories and Human Impact (8 questions)

1. Which articles name specific veterans or pain patients who died by suicide or experienced suicidal ideation following an involuntary opioid taper or discharge from care, and what dates, locations, and dosage details are reported?
2. What first-person or family-member accounts describe the moment a patient was "cut off" — the notification method (letter, appointment, pharmacy refusal), the stated reason, and what happened in the 90 days afterward?
3. Which articles document patients turning to illicit fentanyl, heroin, or street pills specifically because a legal prescription ended, and do any include toxicology or coroner findings?
4. What accounts describe patients using kratom or 7-OH products as a self-managed replacement after losing prescription access, and what outcomes (positive, negative, mixed) do they report?
5. Which stories feature patients with objectively documented conditions (CRPS, adhesive arachnoiditis, sickle cell, post-surgical, cancer survivorship) who were tapered under policies designed for a different population?
6. What articles describe "pharmacy crawls" — patients unable to fill legitimate prescriptions due to wholesaler caps, red-flag algorithms, or pharmacist refusal — and which chains or distributors are named?
7. Which articles document caregivers, spouses, or children describing the household impact of a family member's forced taper (job loss, bedbound status, marital breakdown)?
8. Are there any longitudinal follow-ups — the same patient covered more than once over months or years — and what changed between installments?

### B. Policy Implementation and Consequences (7 questions)

9. Which states enacted morphine-milligram-equivalent (MME) prescribing caps or day-supply limits, in what years, and what post-implementation outcomes (overdose rates, prescribing volume, patient complaints) does coverage report?
10. How is the VA Opioid Safety Initiative (launched 2013) described in terms of measurable effects — percentage reduction in prescribing, number of patients tapered, and any officially acknowledged harms?
11. Which articles cover the 2019 FDA safety communication warning against abrupt tapering and sudden discontinuation, and do any document whether prescriber behavior actually changed afterward?
12. What coverage exists of the CDC's 2022 revised Clinical Practice Guideline — specifically its admission that the 2016 guideline was "misapplied" — and which misapplications (hard caps, involuntary tapers, dismissals) does it enumerate?
13. Which articles document law-enforcement or DEA actions against prescribers (raids, license actions, prosecutions) followed by mass patient abandonment, and how many patients were displaced in each case?
14. What coverage exists of prescription drug monitoring program (PDMP) algorithms or NarxCare-style risk scores leading to denials of care, and are any specific vendors or scoring systems named?
15. Which policies are credited in coverage with *reducing* harm (naloxone access laws, buprenorphine X-waiver removal, safe-supply pilots), and what evidence is cited?

### C. Evidence and Guideline Controversies (6 questions)

16. Which articles critique the evidence base of the 2016 CDC guideline or the VA/DoD opioid therapy guideline, and which named researchers or clinicians (e.g., Stefan Kertesz, Beth Darnall) are quoted making those critiques?
17. How is the GRADE evidence-rating system discussed — specifically the criticism that "low-quality evidence" ratings for long-term opioid therapy were converted into strong policy mandates?
18. What articles cover the Oliva et al. (2020) VA study or similar research linking opioid discontinuation to overdose and suicide, and how do officials respond to those findings in the same coverage?
19. Which articles quote guideline authors or CDC/VA officials defending the guidelines, and what specific counter-evidence do they invoke?
20. What coverage examines conflicts of interest on either side — pharma funding of pain-advocacy groups, or advocacy-org influence (e.g., PROP-affiliated figures) on guideline committees?
21. Which articles discuss the distinction between dependence and addiction (opioid use disorder), and where does coverage conflate the two in describing tapered patients?

### D. Veterans and the VA System (6 questions)

22. Which specific VA medical centers or VISNs are named in connection with patient harm following tapers, and what dates and patient counts are reported?
23. What coverage exists of veteran suicides on or near VA property (parking-lot suicides), and do any articles connect specific cases to pain-care denials or tapers?
24. How has the VA officially responded when coverage linked its opioid policies to veteran suicides — denial, internal review, congressional testimony, policy revision?
25. Which congressional hearings, GAO reports, or VA OIG investigations into pain management and taper practices are covered, and what were their findings and dates?
26. What articles compare VA prescribing metrics (the Opioid Prescribing Dashboard, facility-level rates) with veteran-reported access to care?
27. Which veteran service organizations (VFW, DAV, Wounded Warrior Project) or veteran-specific advocacy voices appear in coverage, and what positions do they take?

### E. Kratom and 7-OH Policy (6 questions)

28. What reasons do articles report for kratom use — pain self-management, opioid withdrawal, replacement after prescription loss, energy/mood — and which reasons dominate by article count?
29. Which states banned kratom (e.g., Alabama, Arkansas, Indiana, Rhode Island, Vermont, Wisconsin) versus which passed Kratom Consumer Protection Acts, and what outcomes does coverage report for each approach?
30. How is the 2016 DEA emergency-scheduling attempt and its withdrawal after public comment covered, and which scientists' or congress members' objections are quoted?
31. How does coverage distinguish (or fail to distinguish) leaf kratom from concentrated 7-hydroxymitragynine products, and when does "7-OH" first appear as a distinct term in the dataset?
32. What coverage exists of the FDA's 2025 push to schedule 7-OH — which industry factions supported versus opposed it, and how are vendor and trade-association positions (e.g., American Kratom Association vs. 7-OH sellers) characterized?
33. Which articles attribute deaths to kratom or 7-OH, what does the underlying toxicology reportedly show (sole agent vs. polydrug), and who is quoted disputing the attribution?

### F. Data and Surveillance Limitations (5 questions)

34. What articles discuss limitations of FAERS (voluntary reporting, no denominator, duplicate reports) when adverse-event counts are used to justify policy action?
35. How is poison-control-center data (NPDS) used in kratom/7-OH coverage, and do articles note that call volume is not incidence?
36. Which articles examine postmortem toxicology practices — polydrug findings reported as single-substance deaths, metabolite misidentification, or medical-examiner inconsistency across counties?
37. What coverage addresses the absence of denominators — user-population estimates for kratom, or counts of stable long-term opioid patients — when risk claims are made?
38. Which articles document gaming or artifacts in prescribing metrics (e.g., facilities improving dashboard numbers by discharging patients rather than improving care)?

### G. Cross-Cutting Analytical Questions (4 questions)

39. Whose perspective is systematically absent — e.g., are currently tapered patients quoted less often than officials, and are people who use drugs quoted at all in scheduling stories?
40. How did coverage volume and framing shift in the 30 days after each major policy event (2016 CDC guideline, 2016 DEA kratom notice, 2019 FDA taper warning, 2022 CDC revision, 2025 7-OH action)?
41. What direct contradictions exist between official statements and patient accounts within the same article or the same news cycle, and how does the article adjudicate them?
42. Which narratives appear in national coverage but not local coverage, and vice versa — e.g., do local outlets report individual pharmacy denials that national outlets aggregate away?

## OUTPUT 2: Article Classification Framework

### 2.1 Tag Set (12 tags)

| Tag | Definition | Apply when the article… |
|---|---|---|
| `TAPER_HARM` | Documented adverse outcome attributed to dose reduction or discontinuation | Names a harm (withdrawal, destabilization, overdose, suicide, ER visit) causally linked to a taper |
| `VETERAN_SUICIDE` | Veteran suicide or suicidal ideation in a pain-care/VA context | Involves a veteran AND suicide/ideation; co-tag with `TAPER_HARM` if taper-linked |
| `GUIDELINE_CRITIQUE` | Challenge to the evidence base or application of CDC/VA/state guidelines | Quotes critics, cites contrary studies, or reports guideline "misapplication" |
| `GUIDELINE_DEFENSE` | Official or expert defense of restrictive guidelines/policies | Quotes officials or researchers defending restrictions (counter-evidence tag) |
| `KRATOM_POLICY` | Kratom or 7-OH regulatory action or debate | Covers bans, KCPAs, scheduling attempts, FDA/DEA actions, import alerts |
| `PATIENT_ABANDONMENT` | Loss of care access without clinical justification | Prescriber closure/raid, patient dismissal, refusal to accept legacy patients |
| `STIGMA` | Stigmatizing framing or documented discrimination | "Drug-seeker" labeling, chart flags, dismissive treatment, loaded language in the article itself |
| `DISPLACEMENT` | Movement to illicit or gray-market supply after legal access ended | Prescription → street opioids, or prescription → kratom/7-OH substitution |
| `DATA_LIMITATION` | Critique or misuse of surveillance data | FAERS, NPDS, PDMP scores, toxicology attribution, missing denominators |
| `PERSONAL_ACCOUNT` | First-person or family-member narrative is central | The story is built around one identifiable patient/family (vs. aggregate reporting) |
| `POLICY_TENSION` | Conflict between policies, agencies, or stated goals | e.g., FDA taper warning vs. state MME caps; addiction-treatment goals vs. pain-care goals |
| `SCOPE_LOCAL` / `SCOPE_NATIONAL` | Geographic scope pair | Local/regional outlet or single-state focus vs. national outlet/multi-state framing (exactly one required) |

### 2.2 JSON Tagging Template

```json
{
  "article_id": "mediacloud-000000",
  "title": "",
  "outlet": "",
  "date": "YYYY-MM-DD",
  "url": "",
  "scope": "SCOPE_LOCAL",
  "tags": ["TAPER_HARM", "PERSONAL_ACCOUNT"],
  "tag_evidence": {
    "TAPER_HARM": "One sentence: what in the article justifies this tag.",
    "PERSONAL_ACCOUNT": "Named patient/family and their role in the piece."
  },
  "geography": {"state": "", "locality": "", "va_facility": ""},
  "policy_events_referenced": ["CDC_2016_GUIDELINE"],
  "stance_signal": "access_sympathetic | restriction_sympathetic | mixed | neutral_report",
  "confidence": "high | medium | low",
  "tagged_by": "machine_drafted",
  "review_status": "pending"
}
```

### 2.3 Application Instructions

1. **Tag from the article text only.** If the justification requires outside knowledge, don't apply the tag; note it in `tag_evidence` as a candidate.
2. **Every tag requires an evidence sentence.** A tag without a `tag_evidence` entry is invalid — this is your audit trail and makes machine-applied tags reviewable later.
3. **Multi-tagging is expected.** Most substantive articles carry 2–4 tags. A veteran-suicide-after-taper story is `VETERAN_SUICIDE` + `TAPER_HARM` + likely `PERSONAL_ACCOUNT`.
4. **Exactly one scope tag** per article. If a national outlet covers one town's story, scope follows the *framing* (usually national) and `geography` captures the place.
5. **`GUIDELINE_DEFENSE` is mandatory when present.** Do not skip counter-evidence — an advocacy portfolio that can't cite the opposing case accurately is weaker, not stronger.
6. **`stance_signal` describes the article, not you.** It records the piece's framing so you can later measure coverage balance; it is not an endorsement.
7. **Machine-applied tags start as `review_status: "pending"`.** Only flip to `approved` after human spot-check — consistent with your trust-layer rule that machine output never masquerades as human judgment.

## OUTPUT 3: Evidence Extraction Template

Use one record per article that survives tagging triage (i.e., carries at least one substantive tag).

```json
{
  "metadata": {
    "article_id": "", "title": "", "outlet": "", "author": "",
    "date": "YYYY-MM-DD", "url": "", "scope": ""
  },
  "core_summary": "2–3 sentences: what happened, to whom, and why it matters for policy.",
  "key_individuals": [
    {"name": "", "role": "patient | family | clinician | official | researcher | advocate | industry", "affiliation": "", "position_taken": ""}
  ],
  "key_data_points": [
    {"datum": "e.g., '90 MME cap effective 2018-07-01' or 'tapered from 180 to 0 MME in 21 days'",
     "source_in_article": "who/what the article attributes it to",
     "verifiable_against": "primary source URL if the article links one, else null"}
  ],
  "direct_quotes": [
    {"quote": "", "speaker": "", "context": "", "paragraph_ref": ""}
  ],
  "policy_relevance": {
    "advocacy_claims_supported": ["which portfolio arguments this evidence supports"],
    "advocacy_claims_undermined": ["be honest — what it cuts against"],
    "policy_events_referenced": []
  },
  "tags": [],
  "extraction_provenance": {
    "extracted_by": "machine_drafted",
    "extraction_date": "",
    "human_verified": false,
    "verification_note": null
  }
}
```

**Rules:**
- `key_data_points` must be specific: numbers, dates, dosages, counts, facility names. "Many patients were affected" is not a data point.
- 2–5 `direct_quotes` maximum; capture speaker and context so quotes can't be orphaned from attribution. Quotes are stored for *your* verification and citation workflow — always re-check against the live article before any is used in a published report.
- `advocacy_claims_undermined` may not be left empty by default. If genuinely nothing cuts against your position, write `"none identified"` — forcing the field keeps extraction honest.
- Nothing extracted here is citable until `human_verified: true` with a typed `verification_note` — the same invariant as your `verify.py` trust layer.

## OUTPUT 4: Theme and Pattern Identification Guide

### 4.1 Frequency analysis
- Count articles per tag, per tag-combination, and per named entity (agency, facility, substance, person).
- Rank tag co-occurrence: `TAPER_HARM` × `VETERAN_SUICIDE`, `KRATOM_POLICY` × `DATA_LIMITATION`, etc. Co-occurrence pairs are where arguments live.
- Flag entities appearing ≥10 times for dedicated entity dossiers (feeds your knowledge-graph brain directly).

### 4.2 Temporal patterns
- Plot monthly article counts per tag against a fixed event timeline: CDC guideline (Mar 2016), DEA kratom notice (Aug–Oct 2016), FDA taper warning (Apr 2019), CDC revision (Nov 2022), 7-OH scheduling push (2025).
- For each event, compare framing in the 30 days before vs. after (stance_signal ratio). Spikes without framing shifts suggest wire-service amplification; spikes *with* shifts suggest genuine narrative change.
- Identify "first mention" dates for key terms (7-OH, forced taper, patient abandonment, NarxCare) to date narrative emergence.

### 4.3 Geographic patterns
- Map article counts by state; overlay policy status (MME cap states, kratom-ban states, KCPA states).
- Look for coverage-policy mismatches: states with aggressive policies but thin coverage are evidence *gaps*, not evidence of no harm.
- Track named VA facilities across articles — repeat mentions of the same facility across time are pattern evidence, not anecdote.

### 4.4 Narrative patterns
- Catalog recurring arcs: "crackdown → displacement → death," "ban → gray market → potency escalation," "guideline → misapplication → official walk-back."
- For each arc, list which articles instantiate it with named cases. An arc with 15 instantiations across 8 states in 6 years is a structural claim; one with 2 is an anecdote.
- Note which arcs officials acknowledge (the CDC's own 2022 misapplication language) versus which appear only in patient accounts — acknowledged arcs are your strongest material.

### 4.5 Gap identification
- Perspectives: count quoted-source types per topic (officials vs. clinicians vs. patients vs. people who use drugs). Systematic absence is itself a reportable finding.
- Evidence: list claims coverage repeats without ever citing a primary source — these become your verification to-do list, not portfolio content.
- Geography and demographics: which populations (rural, incarcerated, sickle-cell patients, non-English-language coverage) are under-covered relative to known exposure?

## OUTPUT 5: Policy Advocacy Report Template

### 1. Executive Summary (1–2 pages)
- 3–5 top-line findings, each stated in one sentence with an article count and date range behind it (e.g., "N articles across M states between 2016–2025 document involuntary tapers followed by documented harm").
- One paragraph on methodology and one on limitations — leading with limitations builds credibility with policy audiences.

### 2. Patient Impact Evidence
- 4–6 fully verified case examples (named or de-identified per source article), each with: circumstance, policy mechanism involved, outcome, and citation.
- Aggregate table: harms by type, by state, by year.
- **Inclusion rule:** only cases where `human_verified: true` and the primary article was re-opened at report time. Unverified material goes to Appendix C as "reported but unverified."

### 3. Policy Implementation Analysis
- Per policy (MME caps, OSI, kratom bans, KCPAs, 7-OH scheduling): stated goal → measured or reported outcome → gap between them.
- Explicit "what worked" subsection — reforms coverage credits with reducing harm. Advocacy that only catalogs failure reads as grievance; contrast is persuasion.

### 4. Evidence Controversies
- Side-by-side treatment: the claim, the data behind it, the documented limitation of that data, and the strongest version of the opposing reading.
- Cover both directions: restriction-justifying data misuse (FAERS counts, toxicology attribution) *and* any weak claims circulating on the access-advocacy side.

### 5. Recommendations
- Each recommendation must trace to specific findings sections by number — no recommendation without an evidentiary chain.
- Distinguish: (a) supported by verified evidence, (b) supported by reported-but-unverified patterns warranting investigation, (c) precautionary. Label honestly.

### 6. Appendices
- A: Full methodology (dataset provenance, tagging framework, verification protocol, this document).
- B: Source index — every cited article with metadata and verification status.
- C: Reported-but-unverified material.
- D: Key quotes with full attribution and context.
- E: Timeline of policy events against coverage volume.

## How This Framework Plugs Into Your Existing Pipeline

- **Tags → extractor labels.** The 12 tags map cleanly onto node labels already in your `extractor.py` ontology (`Systemic_Failure`, `Data_Artifact`, `Vulnerable_Population`, `Policy`, etc.). Tagging can run as a lightweight first pass before full graph extraction — cheap triage of 18,000 articles down to the subset worth deep extraction.
- **Extraction records → trust layer.** Output 3's `extraction_provenance` block mirrors your `analysis_models.py` origin-tagging: everything starts `machine_drafted`, nothing is citable until human-verified with typed testimony, and the report template's inclusion rules enforce the whitelist principle from your README.
- **Questions → NotebookLM and graph queries.** Output 1's questions work verbatim in NotebookLM against your archive.org datasets, and most translate directly into Cypher once the graph is populated (e.g., Q22 is a match on `VA facility` nodes with `LEADS_TO`/`TAPER_HARM`-adjacent edges).
